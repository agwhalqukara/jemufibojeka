Nissan Frontier 2022 Price, Features, Engine, Mileage, Top Speed &amp; 0-60 mph

July 2024 · 2 minute read

Contents

Overview

The 2022 Nissan Frontier is now completely changed and looks rugged as well than previous generation. Interior is upgraded with latest tech and gizmos. The off-road oriented Pro-4X variant is refined on highway and perfectly insulated from wind and road noise. Its steering wheel feedback is not so engaging at lower speeds. Frontier’s back row is quite compact. Frontier is powered by 310 HP V6 engine mated 9-speed automatic transmission with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive system.

What’s new For 2022?

The 3rd generation Frontier gets revised suspension setup but still the frame is same as previous.

Pricing & features

It offers many desirable features such as 8.0-inch or optional 9.0-inch infotainment system with android Auto and Apple CarPlay, wireless charging and subscription based Wi-Fi hotspot, 9-speaker stereo system, heated front seats and 6-way power adjustable driver’s seat.

Frontier comes with many optional features such as lane departure warning, adaptive cruise control and blind spot monitoring with rear cross traffic alert.

Dimensions & Weight

  • Length: 210.2-inch
  • Width: 73.0-inch
  • Height: 71.7-inch
  • Wheelbase: 126.0-inch
  • Curb weight: 4343lbs

    • Engine, Top Speed, 0-60 mph & Performance

    The 2022 Nissan Frontier is powered by 3.8-litre NA V6 engine generates 310 HP and 281 pound-feet of torque paired with 9-speed automatic transmission with rear-wheel or four-wheel drivetrain. Frontier can go from 0-60 mph in just 8.0 Seconds and have a top speed of 130 mph. If you want true off-roading fun then opt a top end Pro-4X variant which attracts the adventure seekers. It has electronic locking rear differential, beefier suspension with independent front suspension with stabilizer bar. It can tow 6720 pounds which is quite less than the competition such as Jeep Gladiator, GMC Canyon and Chevrolet Colorado. It has payload capacity of 1610 pounds.

    Fuel efficiency | Mileage

    Well according to EPA, the Nissan Frontier with rear-wheel drive system delivers 18 mpg in city and 24 mpg on highway while the all-wheel drive variant manages to earn rating of 17 mpg in city and 22 mpg on highway.
